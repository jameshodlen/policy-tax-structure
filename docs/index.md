# The American Tax Landscape

**Where does the money come from? Who pays? What happens next?**

Tax Structure Watch is the first platform to comprehensively map the distributional impact of every state's tax structure using primary-source data with genuinely nonpartisan framing. We present competing analytical frameworks side by side — with transparent discussion of their differing assumptions — linked back to primary federal and state data sources.

This platform does not tell you what to conclude. It shows where the money comes from, who pays it, and what happens next.

---

## What Makes This Different

!!! info "Competing Frameworks, Not Competing Conclusions"
    Most tax analysis comes pre-packaged with a political lean. The Tax Foundation (center-right) emphasizes broad bases, low rates, and competitive structures. ITEP (center-left) emphasizes distributional equity and effective tax burdens by income level. **Both are rigorous. Both are incomplete.** We present them side by side so you can evaluate the evidence yourself.

### Three Commitments

1. **Primary Sources Only** — Every data point traces back to Census Bureau, IRS, BEA, or state revenue departments. No secondary summaries without verification.

2. **Transparent Methodology** — Every calculated metric has a [methodology page](methodology/index.md) explaining what data we use, what assumptions we make, and what we include and exclude.

3. **The Nonpartisan Test** — Can a Republican legislator in Iowa and a Democratic legislator in Minnesota both use this platform productively? If yes, the framing is right.

## The Current Landscape (Early 2026)

The American state tax landscape is undergoing its most significant structural transformation in decades:

- **24 states** now have flat or no individual income tax, up from 19 in 2021. Eight states converted from graduated to flat between 2021 and 2025.
- **Counter-trends** are emerging: Maryland enacted new high-earner brackets plus a digital services tax; Massachusetts voters approved a 9% top rate on income above $1M; Washington imposed a 9.9% capital gains tax.
- **36 jurisdictions** adopted pass-through entity tax (PTET) workarounds to the federal SALT cap — creating a two-tier system where business owners can largely circumvent the cap while wage earners cannot.
- The **10 most regressive** state tax systems (per ITEP) are all characterized by heavy sales and excise tax reliance with weak or absent income taxes.
- Only **6 states plus DC** reduce inequality through their tax codes (per ITEP): California, Maine, Minnesota, New Jersey, New York, and Vermont.

These trends are reshaping who pays for state services, how much they pay, and what those services look like. This platform tracks it all.
