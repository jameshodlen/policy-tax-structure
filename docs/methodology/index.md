# Methodology

## Core Approach: Follow the Money

Tax Structure Watch traces financial flows through state tax systems using primary-sourced, publicly available data. Every analysis starts with the same question: **Where does the money come from, who pays it, and what happens next?**

We do not begin with a theory of what good tax policy looks like. We begin with the data — Census Bureau revenue collections, IRS income distributions, BEA economic context — and present what the numbers show alongside competing interpretations of what they mean.

## Standards

- **All data from public, primary sources** — Census Bureau, IRS Statistics of Income, Bureau of Economic Analysis, state revenue departments. No secondary summaries without verification against the original.
- **Automated, reproducible pipelines** — Every dataset is fetched, processed, and validated through documented Python scripts. Anyone can audit the data trail.
- **State-level comparisons anchored in Wisconsin and Minnesota** — Our anchor comparison for validating methodology before scaling to all 50 states.
- **Transparent methodology with cited sources** — Every calculated metric on this platform links to a methodology page explaining data sources, assumptions, inclusions, exclusions, and limitations.
- **Competing frameworks presented side by side** — We present both the Tax Foundation (center-right) and ITEP (center-left) analytical frameworks with transparent discussion of their differences.

## The Nonpartisan Test

Before any content goes live, we audit every page against this standard:

1. Does it cite both center-right and center-left perspectives where relevant?
2. Does it present competing interpretations without editorializing?
3. Does every claim link to a primary source?
4. Could a Republican legislator in Iowa and a Democratic legislator in Minnesota both find value without feeling attacked?

If the answer to any of these is no, the page goes back for revision.
