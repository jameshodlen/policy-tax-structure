# Washington Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Washington does not levy an individual income tax, a 6.5% state sales tax. ITEP ranks Washington's tax system among the 10 most regressive in the nation, with lower-income households paying a higher effective tax rate than wealthier residents. The Tax Foundation ranks Washington 16th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax

Washington does not levy an individual income tax. This places it among nine states that forgo this revenue source, relying instead on sales taxes, property taxes, and other revenue streams.

## Historical Context

- **1935**: Business and Occupation (B&O) tax enacted — gross receipts tax, not income tax
- **1966**: Sales tax rate set at 4.5%, reaching current 6.5% by 1983
- **2010**: Income tax ballot initiative (I-1098) rejected by voters
- **2021**: Capital gains tax of 7% enacted on gains over $250,000
- **2023**: State Supreme Court upholds capital gains tax (classified as excise tax)
- **2025**: Capital gains tax rate increased to 9.9%
