# Oregon Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Oregon employs a graduated individual income tax with 4 brackets and a top rate of 9.9%, and a 7.6% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Oregon 41st on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $4,050 | 4.75% |
| $4,050 – $10,200 | 6.75% |
| $10,200 – $125,000 | 8.75% |
| Over $125,000 | 9.9% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County assessors, Measure 50 limits assessed value growth |
| **Estate Tax** | Yes — $1,000,000 exemption |
| **Notable Credits** | Earned Income Tax Credit (state), Oregon Kids' Credit, Working Family Household and Dependent Care Credit |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Rolling conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1930**: Individual income tax enacted (one of earliest western states)
- **1990**: Measure 5 — caps property taxes; shifts school funding to state income tax
- **2000**: 'Kicker' enshrined in constitution — tax revenue 2% above forecast must be refunded
- **2020**: Corporate Activity Tax takes effect — Oregon's first broad-based business tax beyond corporate income tax
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
