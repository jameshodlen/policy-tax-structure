# Connecticut Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Connecticut employs a graduated individual income tax with 7 brackets and a top rate of 6.99%, a 6.35% state sales tax, and a 7.5% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Connecticut 51st on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $10,000 | 2.0% |
| $10,000 – $50,000 | 4.5% |
| $50,000 – $100,000 | 5.5% |
| $100,000 – $200,000 | 6.0% |
| $200,000 – $250,000 | 6.5% |
| $250,000 – $500,000 | 6.9% |
| Over $500,000 | 6.99% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | 169 municipalities. Property assessed at 70% of fair market value. Among the highest effective property tax rates in the US. |
| **Estate Tax** | Yes — $13,990,000 exemption |
| **Notable Credits** | State EITC (40% of federal + $250 for families with children), Property Tax Credit (up to $300), Angel Investor Tax Credit |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Rolling conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1991**: Individual income tax enacted at flat 4.5% under Gov. Weicker; sales tax cut from 8% to 6%
- **1996**: Dual-bracket rate structure introduced (replacing flat rate)
- **2009**: Top rate raised to 6.99% on income over $500,000; bracket count expanded
- **2024**: PTET made elective (was mandatory). Bottom two marginal rates reduced. Estate tax exemption raised to near-federal levels.
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: High
