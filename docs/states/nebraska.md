# Nebraska Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Nebraska employs a graduated individual income tax with 4 brackets and a top rate of 5.2%, a 5.5% state sales tax, and a 5.2% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Nebraska 29th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $3,700 | 2.46% |
| $3,700 – $22,170 | 3.51% |
| $22,170 – $35,730 | 5.01% |
| Over $35,730 | 5.2% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County assessors. State Property Tax Administrator provides oversight. Property assessed at actual value. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | Community Development Assistance Act credit, Nebraska Advantage Act credits |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |

## Historical Context

- **1967**: Individual income tax, corporate income tax, and sales tax all enacted simultaneously
- **2023**: LB 754 — largest income tax cut in state history; top rate phased from 6.64% to 3.99% by 2027
- **2025**: Debate over pausing scheduled rate cuts due to revenue shortfalls
