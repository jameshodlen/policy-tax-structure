# Missouri Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Missouri employs a graduated individual income tax with 8 brackets and a top rate of 4.7%, a 4.225% state sales tax, and a 4.0% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Missouri 15th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $1,207 | 0.0% |
| $1,207 – $2,414 | 2.0% |
| $2,414 – $3,621 | 2.5% |
| $3,621 – $4,828 | 3.0% |
| $4,828 – $6,035 | 3.5% |
| $6,035 – $7,242 | 4.0% |
| $7,242 – $8,449 | 4.5% |
| Over $8,449 | 4.7% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County assessors. Reassessment every two years. Classified system with different assessment ratios. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | 100% capital gains exclusion (new 2025), Property tax credit for seniors |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Rolling conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1917**: Individual income tax enacted
- **1937**: Sales tax enacted
- **1980**: Hancock Amendment — caps state revenue as percentage of personal income, requires voter approval for tax increases
- **2014**: Phased income tax rate cuts enacted; top rate gradually reduced from 6% toward 4.7%
- **2025**: Top rate reduced to 4.7% via revenue trigger. 100% capital gains exclusion enacted.
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: Moderate
