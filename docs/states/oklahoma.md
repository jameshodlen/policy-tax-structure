# Oklahoma Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Oklahoma employs a graduated individual income tax with 6 brackets and a top rate of 4.75%, a 4.5% state sales tax, and a 4.0% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Oklahoma 20th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $1,000 | 0.25% |
| $1,000 – $2,500 | 0.75% |
| $2,500 – $3,750 | 1.75% |
| $3,750 – $4,900 | 2.75% |
| $4,900 – $7,200 | 3.75% |
| Over $7,200 | 4.75% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County assessors. State Equalization Board provides oversight. Fair cash value assessment. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | Oklahoma Earned Income Credit, Child care/child tax credit, Investment/new jobs credit |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |

## Historical Context

- **1915**: Individual income tax enacted
- **1933**: State constitution amended to prohibit state-level property taxation
- **2009**: Top rate cut from 7% to 5.5% over 2004-2009 period
- **2025**: HB 2764: brackets consolidated from 6 to 3; top rate to 4.5% in 2026 with trigger toward elimination
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: Low
