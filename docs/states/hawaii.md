# Hawaii Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Hawaii employs a graduated individual income tax with 12 brackets and a top rate of 11.0%, a 4.0% state sales tax, and a 6.4% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Hawaii 43rd on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County-administered. Each county sets own rates and classifications. Assessed at 100% market value. |
| **Estate Tax** | Yes — $5,490,000 exemption |
| **Notable Credits** | Refundable EITC, Renewable energy/solar credit, PTE credit |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Selective conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1901**: Income taxes among earliest in US territory
- **1935**: General Excise Tax (GET) enacted at 4% — functions like a sales tax but levied on businesses
- **1957**: Statehood; existing territorial tax system carried forward
- **2024**: Act 46 (Green Affordability Plan II): Widened all 12 income brackets; doubled standard deduction. Act 50 reduced PTET rate.
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Selective conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: High
