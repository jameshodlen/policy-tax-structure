# North Dakota Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

North Dakota employs a graduated individual income tax with 3 brackets and a top rate of 2.5%, a 5.0% state sales tax, and a 4.31% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks North Dakota 22nd on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $55,975 | 0.0% |
| $55,975 – $269,100 | 1.95% |
| Over $269,100 | 2.5% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County and city assessors. State Tax Commissioner provides oversight. True and full value assessment. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | Property tax credit, Renaissance zone credits |
| **PTET Status** | Not adopted |
| **Federal Conformity** | Rolling conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1919**: Individual income tax enacted (first among neighboring states)
- **1935**: Sales tax enacted at 2%
- **2023**: HB 1158 — largest income tax cut in state history; brackets compressed from 5 to 3; first $55,975 tax-free
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Not adopted
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: Low
