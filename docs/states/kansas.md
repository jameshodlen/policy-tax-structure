# Kansas Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Kansas employs a graduated individual income tax with 2 brackets and a top rate of 5.58%, a 6.5% state sales tax, and a 7.0% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Kansas 27th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

## Historical Context

- **1933**: Individual income tax enacted
- **1937**: Sales tax enacted at 2%
- **2012**: 'Kansas Experiment' — top rate cut from 6.45% to 4.9%; pass-through income exempted; massive revenue shortfall followed
- **2017**: Legislature overrides veto to repeal 2012 tax cuts; top rate raised to 5.7%
- **2024**: SB 1: Consolidated 3 brackets to 2; top rate reduced to 5.58%; Social Security fully exempt
