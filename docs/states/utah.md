# Utah Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Utah employs a flat individual income tax at 4.5%, a 4.7% state sales tax, and a 4.5% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Utah 10th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County assessors. State Tax Commission provides oversight. Fair market value assessment. Truth-in-taxation process limits rate increases. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | Taxpayer tax credit (nonrefundable, effectively creates 0% bracket), At-home parent credit, Retirement tax credit |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Rolling conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1931**: Individual income tax enacted
- **1933**: Sales tax enacted
- **2008**: Income tax converted from graduated brackets to flat 5.0% rate
- **2025**: Flat rate reduced to 4.50% (from 4.55%) — fourth consecutive annual cut via HB 106
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: Low
