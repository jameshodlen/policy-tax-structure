# Alabama Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Alabama employs a graduated individual income tax with 3 brackets and a top rate of 5.0%, a 4.0% state sales tax, and a 6.5% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Alabama 21st on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $500 | 2.0% |
| $500 – $3,000 | 4.0% |
| Over $3,000 | 5.0% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County-administered; among the lowest effective rates in the US (~0.36%). State oversight by Alabama Department of Revenue. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | Federal income tax deduction (unique among states), Overtime pay exemption (expired June 2025) |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Rolling conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1933**: Individual income tax enacted with graduated rates (2% to 5%)
- **1936**: Sales tax enacted at 2%
- **1963**: Constitutional amendment increased allowable corporate income tax rate to 5%
- **2021**: Single sales factor apportionment adopted for business income
- **2025**: Grocery sales tax reduced; PTET election process simplified
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: Moderate
