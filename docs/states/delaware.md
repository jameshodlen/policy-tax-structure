# Delaware Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Delaware employs a graduated individual income tax with 7 brackets and a top rate of 6.6%, and a 8.7% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Delaware 30th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $2,000 | 0.0% |
| $2,000 – $5,000 | 2.2% |
| $5,000 – $10,000 | 3.9% |
| $10,000 – $20,000 | 4.8% |
| $20,000 – $25,000 | 5.2% |
| $25,000 – $60,000 | 5.55% |
| Over $60,000 | 6.6% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County-administered (3 counties). Relatively low effective rates (~0.48%). Gross receipts tax on businesses serves as partial substitute for sales tax. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | No sales tax, Social Security benefits fully exempt, Itemized deduction for charitable contributions |
| **PTET Status** | Not adopted |
| **Federal Conformity** | Rolling conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1970**: Modern personal income tax enacted (graduated rates, 2.2%-6.6%)
- **2018**: Estate tax repealed
- **2025**: Selectively decoupled from OBBBA provisions. No PTET enacted (one of five income-tax states without one).
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Not adopted
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
