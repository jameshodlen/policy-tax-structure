# Massachusetts Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Massachusetts employs a flat individual income tax at 5.0%, a 6.25% state sales tax, and a 8.0% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Massachusetts 37th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | Local assessors value at full and fair cash value. Proposition 2-1/2 limits levy growth to 2.5% annually. |
| **Estate Tax** | Yes — $2,000,000 exemption |
| **Notable Credits** | EITC, Child/dependent care credit, Lead paint removal credit, Film production credit |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Selective conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1916**: Individual income tax enacted (among earliest states)
- **1966**: Sales tax enacted at 3%
- **1980**: Proposition 2-1/2 enacted — caps property tax levies at 2.5% of assessed value
- **2000**: Voters approve reducing income tax rate to 5% (took until 2020 to fully implement)
- **2023**: 'Millionaire\'s tax' (4% surtax on income over ~$1M) takes effect via 2022 ballot measure
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Selective conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: High
