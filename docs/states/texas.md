# Texas Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Texas does not levy an individual income tax, a 6.25% state sales tax. ITEP ranks Texas's tax system among the 10 most regressive in the nation, with lower-income households paying a higher effective tax rate than wealthier residents. The Tax Foundation ranks Texas 14th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax

## Historical Context

- **1961**: Sales tax enacted at 2%, gradually raised to 6.25% by 1990
- **1991**: Franchise tax reformed to include partnerships and corporations
- **1993**: Constitutional amendment prohibits personal income tax without voter approval
- **2006**: Franchise tax replaced with Margins Tax (reformed business tax)
- **2023**: $18 billion property tax relief package signed. Franchise tax threshold raised.
