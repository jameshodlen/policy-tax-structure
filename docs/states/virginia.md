# Virginia Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

Virginia employs a graduated individual income tax with 4 brackets and a top rate of 5.75%, a 4.3% state sales tax, and a 6.0% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks Virginia 23rd on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $3,000 | 2.0% |
| $3,000 – $5,000 | 3.0% |
| $5,000 – $17,000 | 5.0% |
| Over $17,000 | 5.75% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | Local commissioners of the revenue or assessors. State oversight through Department of Taxation. Fair market value assessment. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | EITC (raised to 20% of federal), Land preservation credit, Historic rehabilitation credit, R&D credit |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Selective conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1843**: Individual income tax enacted (one of the earliest states in the nation)
- **1966**: Retail Sales and Use Tax enacted at 2%
- **2025**: Rolling conformity replaced with fixed date. PTET made permanent. EITC raised to 20%. Selective deconformity from OBBBA.
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Selective conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: Moderate
