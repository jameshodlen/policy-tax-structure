# New Hampshire Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

New Hampshire does not levy an individual income tax, relying instead on sales taxes, property taxes, and other revenue sources, and a 7.5% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks New Hampshire 6th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax

New Hampshire does not levy an individual income tax. This places it among nine states that forgo this revenue source, relying instead on sales taxes, property taxes, and other revenue streams.

### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | Local municipal assessors. No statewide property tax. Among the highest effective property tax rates in the nation (~1.86%). |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | No individual income tax, No sales tax |
| **PTET Status** | Not adopted |
| **Federal Conformity** | Static conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1923**: Interest and Dividends (I&D) Tax enacted at 5% (only form of individual income tax)
- **1970**: Business Profits Tax enacted
- **2021**: Legislature passes phase-out of I&D Tax
- **2025**: I&D Tax fully repealed — NH becomes true no-income-tax state
