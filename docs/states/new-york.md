# New York Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

New York employs a graduated individual income tax with 9 brackets and a top rate of 10.9%, a 4.0% state sales tax, and a 7.25% corporate tax rate. New York is one of only six states (plus DC) that ITEP identifies as reducing income inequality through its tax code. The Tax Foundation ranks New York 48th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

## Historical Context

- **1919**: Individual income tax enacted — one of the first states
- **2009**: 'Millionaire's tax' surcharge enacted on high earners
- **2011**: Tax reform restructures brackets, top rate initially set to expire
- **2021**: Top rate raised to 10.9% on income over $25M, new brackets added
- **2023**: Top rate of 10.9% extended through 2027. PTET expanded.
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
- **SALT Cap Impact**: High
