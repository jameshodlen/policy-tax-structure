# West Virginia Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

West Virginia employs a graduated individual income tax with 5 brackets and a top rate of 4.82%, a 6.0% state sales tax, and a 6.5% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks West Virginia 47th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $10,000 | 2.22% |
| $10,000 – $25,000 | 2.96% |
| $25,000 – $40,000 | 3.33% |
| $40,000 – $60,000 | 4.44% |
| Over $60,000 | 4.82% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|

| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | Senior citizen tax credit, Film industry investment credit, Manufacturing investment credit |

## Historical Context

- **1934**: Sales tax enacted
- **1963**: Modern personal income tax enacted
- **2023**: HB 2526 — largest income tax cut in state history (21.25% average reduction); top rate from 6.5% to 5.12%
- **2025**: SB 2033 further reduces rates; five brackets now 2.22%-4.82%. Social Security exemption phasing to 100% by 2026.
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Selective conformity to IRC
