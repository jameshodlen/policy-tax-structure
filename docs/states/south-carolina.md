# South Carolina Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

South Carolina employs a graduated individual income tax with 3 brackets and a top rate of 6.0%, a 6.0% state sales tax, and a 5.0% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks South Carolina 25th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $3,460 | 0.0% |
| $3,460 – $17,330 | 3.0% |
| Over $17,330 | 6.0% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County assessors. Different assessment ratios: 4% for owner-occupied residential, 6% for other residential, 10.5% for commercial. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | Tuition tax credit, Two-wage earner credit, Child and dependent care credit |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Selective conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1922**: Individual income tax first enacted
- **1951**: Sales tax enacted at 3%
- **2022**: Top rate reduced from 7% to 6.5% with triggers for further reductions
- **2025**: Top rate cut to 6.0%. H.4216 proposes flat 3.99% for 2026 with path toward elimination.
