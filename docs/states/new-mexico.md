# New Mexico Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

New Mexico employs a graduated individual income tax with 6 brackets and a top rate of 5.9%, a 5.125% state sales tax, and a 5.9% corporate tax rate. The Tax Foundation ranks New Mexico 39th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

### Income Tax Brackets (Single Filer, 2025)

| Taxable Income | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| $0 – $5,500 | 1.5% |
| $5,500 – $11,000 | 3.2% |
| $11,000 – $16,000 | 4.7% |
| $16,000 – $210,000 | 4.9% |
| $210,000 – $315,000 | 5.3% |
| Over $315,000 | 5.9% |
### Other Key Features

| Category | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Property Tax** | County assessors. State-imposed limits on assessment increases. One-third of current and correct value for residential. |
| **Estate Tax** | None |
| **Notable Credits** | Low Income Comprehensive Tax Rebate, Working Families Tax Credit, Solar Market Development Tax Credit |
| **PTET Status** | Adopted — pass-through entity tax available |
| **Federal Conformity** | Rolling conformity to IRC |

## Historical Context

- **1913**: Personal income tax first imposed
- **1966**: Gross Receipts Tax enacted, replacing Emergency School Tax
- **2022**: GRT rate reduced from 5.125% to 5%, further to 4.875% in 2023
- **2025**: Income tax restructured to 6 brackets (1.5%-5.9%) under HB 252
## Federal Interaction

- **PTET Status**: Adopted — pass-through entity tax available
- **Federal Conformity**: Rolling conformity to IRC
