# California Tax Structure

> **Last updated**: March 2026 | **Data sources**: Census Bureau, BEA, IRS SOI, ITEP, Tax Foundation

## Overview

California employs a graduated individual income tax with 10 brackets and a top rate of 13.3%, a 7.25% state sales tax, and a 8.84% corporate tax rate. California is one of only six states (plus DC) that ITEP identifies as reducing income inequality through its tax code. The Tax Foundation ranks California 49th on its State Tax Competitiveness Index.

## Historical Context

- **1935**: Individual income tax enacted with graduated rates
- **1978**: Proposition 13 passes — caps property tax rate at 1%, limits assessment increases to 2%/year
- **2004**: Mental Health Services Act (Prop 63) adds 1% surcharge on income over $1M
- **2012**: Proposition 30 temporarily raises top rate to 13.3% (highest in nation)
- **2016**: Proposition 55 extends Prop 30 top rates through 2030
- **2024**: Mental Health Services Tax extended. CalEITC expanded.
