# SALT Cap & PTET: A Two-Tier System

!!! info "Research in Progress"
    This analysis is currently under development. The data pipeline is operational
    and source collection is underway. Expected publication: Q2 2026.

## What This Analysis Will Cover

The OBBBA raised the SALT cap from $10,000 to $40,000 (with phase-out above $500,000 AGI). Meanwhile, 36 jurisdictions have adopted pass-through entity taxes (PTETs), which effectively allow business owners to circumvent the SALT cap entirely. This creates a two-tier system where the SALT limitation's bite depends on how you earn your income.

This analysis will quantify the revenue and distributional effects of both the revised SALT cap and the PTET workaround. We will examine who benefits from the higher cap, who benefits from PTETs, and how much state and federal revenue is affected by the interaction of these two provisions. The core question: does the combination of a raised-but-still-capped SALT deduction plus an uncapped PTET pathway create a meaningful equity problem in the federal tax code?

## Data Sources

- IRS Statistics of Income SALT deduction data
- Tax Foundation PTET tracking and analysis
- State revenue department PTET adoption dates and participation data

## Preliminary Findings

36 jurisdictions have adopted pass-through entity tax (PTET) provisions, including: AL, AZ, AR, CA, CO, CT, GA, ID, IL, LA, MD, MA, MN, MS, NJ, NY, NC, OK, OR, RI, SC, WI, and others. Adoption accelerated rapidly after IRS Notice 2020-75 confirmed the deductibility of these entity-level taxes, effectively creating a federally sanctioned workaround to the SALT cap for business owners structured as pass-through entities.
