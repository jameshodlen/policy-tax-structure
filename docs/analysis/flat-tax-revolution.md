# The Flat Tax Revolution

!!! info "Research in Progress"
    This analysis is currently under development. The data pipeline is operational
    and source collection is underway. Expected publication: Q2 2026.

## What This Analysis Will Cover

Since 2021, eight states have converted from graduated to flat income tax structures (Arizona, Georgia, Idaho, Iowa, Kentucky, Mississippi, Montana, North Carolina), joining the existing flat-tax states. Several more have enacted trigger mechanisms to reduce or eliminate their income taxes as revenue targets are met. This represents the most significant structural transformation in state taxation in decades.

This analysis will track each conversion in detail — the legislative path, the rate chosen, the revenue impact, and the distributional consequences. We will compare pre- and post-conversion revenue trends, examine whether trigger mechanisms are fiscally sound or politically convenient, and assess the cumulative effect on the progressivity of state tax systems nationwide.

## Data Sources

- Census Bureau Annual Survey of State and Local Government Tax Collections (historical)
- Tax Foundation state income tax rate tables
- IRS Statistics of Income migration data
- ITEP distributional analysis of state tax systems

## Preliminary Findings

Eight states have converted from graduated to flat income tax structures since 2021:

- **Arizona** — Converted to flat rate in 2023
- **Georgia** — Converted to flat rate in 2024
- **Idaho** — Converted to flat rate in 2023
- **Iowa** — Phased conversion, 2023–2026
- **Kentucky** — Converted to flat rate in 2022
- **Mississippi** — Phased conversion, 2022–2026
- **Montana** — Converted to flat rate in 2024
- **North Carolina** — Flat rate since 2014, with continued rate reductions
