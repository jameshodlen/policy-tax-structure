# The Hidden Budget: Tax Expenditures Across 50 States

!!! info "Research in Progress"
    This analysis is currently under development. The data pipeline is operational
    and source collection is underway. Expected publication: Q3 2026.

## What This Analysis Will Cover

Every state exemption, deduction, and credit represents revenue the state chose not to collect. These "tax expenditures" constitute a hidden budget that often exceeds direct spending on comparable programs. The Volcker Alliance found dramatic variation in how states report (or don't report) these costs. Some states publish comprehensive tax expenditure budgets; others have no systematic reporting at all.

This analysis will catalog tax expenditure reporting practices across all 50 states, compare the scale of tax expenditures to direct appropriations, and highlight sectors — particularly economic development — where tax expenditures have become the dominant policy tool. We will examine which states have adopted best practices in transparency and sunset review, and which operate with minimal accountability for foregone revenue.

The economic development incentive landscape deserves particular scrutiny. State and local incentive awards have grown steadily, and the competitive dynamics that drive them show no signs of slowing.

## Data Sources

- State tax expenditure reports (ITEP directory of state tax expenditure budgets)
- NASBO State Expenditure Report
- Volcker Alliance budget practice grades and transparency assessments

## Preliminary Findings

Economic development incentive awards reached $59.2 billion in 2024, a record high. This figure underscores the scale of revenue states are directing through the tax code rather than through direct appropriations — and the urgency of comprehensive, standardized reporting.
